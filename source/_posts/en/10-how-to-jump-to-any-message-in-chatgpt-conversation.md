## Why This Matters

ChatGPT conversations are linear by design. You type a message, ChatGPT responds, you type again, and so on. The conversation builds downward in a single thread.

This linear format works perfectly for brief exchanges. But for serious work — research sessions, code debugging, draft collaboration, or multi-topic discussions — conversations can quickly grow to 50, 100, or even 200 messages. At that point, the linear format becomes a liability.

Finding a specific message in a long thread means scrolling manually, scanning each message for the topic you remember, and hoping you do not overshoot. This is slow, frustrating, and error-prone.

The alternative is jump navigation: an outline of all messages that you can scan and click to jump instantly to any point in the conversation.

---

## How Jump Navigation Works

Jump navigation replaces linear scrolling with structured browsing. Instead of scrolling through every message sequentially, you interact with a sidebar outline that shows the full conversation structure.

The outline displays each message as an entry, typically showing:

- The message number or position
- A truncated preview of the message text
- Visual distinction between user messages and assistant responses
- Icons for bookmarked or highlighted messages
- A tree or list structure that is easy to scan

When you click an entry, the conversation view scrolls to that exact message. The experience is similar to clicking a link in a table of contents and jumping to the relevant section of a document.
